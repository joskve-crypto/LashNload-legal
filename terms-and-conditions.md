2. DESCRIPTION AND SCOPE OF THE SERVICE
2.1 Nature of the Service
The Service is a decision-support and documentation tool intended to assist drivers, transport operators, and logistics personnel in estimating cargo securing requirements and documenting securing measures. The Service does not constitute a certified regulatory tool, is not approved by any governmental or competent transport authority, and does not replace professional training, formal qualification, or expert judgment.
All Outputs generated by the Service are estimates only. Users must not rely on any Output as the sole or definitive basis for any cargo securing decision. Any reference to the Service as a "professional tool" refers solely to its intended user group and shall not be construed as a representation of certified accuracy or suitability for safety-critical applications.
2.2 Regulatory Basis
The Service performs calculations based on the mathematical principles set forth in European Standard EN 12195-1 (Load restraining on road vehicles — Safety — Part 1: Calculation of securing forces) as interpreted and implemented at the time of the most recent software update. The Service does not purport to implement EN 12195-1 in its entirety, and Users are expressly advised that:
(a) the transport of Dangerous Goods subject to the ADR Agreement, or exceptional and oversized transports, may be subject to additional or stricter regulatory requirements not addressed by the Service; and
(b) national and local laws may impose requirements that differ from or exceed those reflected in the Service's calculations.
Use of the Service in connection with any such transport is strictly at the User's own risk.
2.3 Calculation Modules
The Service includes interactive calculation tools for the following cargo securing methods:
(a) Tie-Down Lashing (Friction Lashing): Calculates the required Standard Tension Force (STF) and resultant downward force to prevent cargo sliding.
(b) Direct Lashing: Calculates the required Lashing Capacity (LC) for direct restraint of cargo to vehicle anchor points.
(c) Loop Lashing: Calculates the required securing forces for loads restrained by loops around the cargo profile.
2.4 User-Inputted Variables
The Service processes variables inputted exclusively by the User, including, without limitation: cargo mass, friction coefficients, lashing angles (α and β), and trailer structural certifications (EN 12642 Code L and Code XL). The Service does not independently verify, validate, or sanity-check any User-supplied input.
2.5 Hardware Integration and Sensor Limitations
Where accessed via a mobile device, the App may request permission to access the following hardware:
(a) Motion Sensors (Gyroscope/Accelerometer): Used to provide a real-time measurement tool for capturing the physical angles of lashing straps. The User expressly acknowledges that: (i) device sensors are subject to hardware inaccuracies, environmental interference, and drift; (ii) correct placement of the device on the lashing strap is the sole responsibility of the User; (iii) all sensor-derived measurements are indicative only and must be independently verified by the User through visual inspection; and (iv) the Service does not guarantee the accuracy of any angle measurement under any circumstances. The Service expressly does not substitute for such an instrument.
(b) Camera: Used solely to capture photographic evidence of secured cargo for documentation purposes.
